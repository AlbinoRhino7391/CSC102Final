HANAMI DANGO

Tricolored Rice Flour Dumplings

What you will need:

Ingredient Amount
Mochicko Glutinous Rice Flour 1/2 Cup
Hot water 1/4 + a dash
Joshinko Non-Glutinous Rice Flour 1/2 Cup
Sugar 2 Tablespoon
Matcha Powder 1/2 teaspoon
Red Food Coloring 1-2 drops
Wooden Skewers 6

Directions:

  1. Set a medium pot filled 3/4 with water to boil over medium-high heat.
  2. In a medium mixing bowl, stir together Mochicko and hot water until rice flour is dissolved. Add the Joshinkoand sugar and stir until well combined. Gradually stir in dashes of hot water as needed(up to another 1/4 Cup) until texture of dough is soft.
  3. Knead the dough to make it very smooth and divide it into 3 equal segments. Place the segments into 3 seperate bowls and cover with plastic wrap to keep dough from getting dry.
  4. Place the matcha powder in a small bowl and add a dash of hot water. Mix until you create a paste. using a toothpick, add small amounts of the paste to one of the dough balls, kneading it until you get a smooth green color.Wash hands after as to not transfer color to the other dough balls.
  5. Add one drop of red food coloring to a bowl of dough, knead it well to incorporate it evenly. Again wash your hands afterwards as to keep from transferring colors to other dough.
  6. Fill a large bowl half way with ice water.
  7. Remove the plain dough from its bowl, roll into a log. Divide the log into 6 equal bits. Roll each bit into a small ball and lower them into the boiling water. Once they float to the top, let them boil for an additional 2 minutes. Once done, scoop out with a slotted spoon and transfer to the ice bath.
  8. Repeat step 7 with the red dough, then the green dough. Once finished you can discard the boiling water.
  9. Once all dango bits have cooled, thread them onto a skewer with: green on bottom, white in the middle, and pink on top. They are now ready to serve.
