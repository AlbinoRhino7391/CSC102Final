Miso Chasu Ramen

Noodle Soup with Braised Pork and Miso Broth

Serves 2 people

What you will need:

Ingredient Amount
Sesame Oil 1 Tablespoon
Minced Onion 1/4 large Onion
Ginger 1 inch, freshly peeled and minced
Minced Garlic 2 Teaspoons
Ground Pork 1/4 Pound
Miso Paste (red or white) 3 Tablespoon
Chicken Stock 2 Cup
Dashi Stock 1 Cup
Mirin 2 Tablespoon
Green Onions 4 (discard dark green and roots)
Salt and White Pepper add to taste
Ramen or Chuka Soba Noodles 6 Ounces
Chasu Pork (Please see page) 10-14 Slices
Menma (Please see page) 1 Cup
Ajitsuke Tamago (Please see page) 2
Narutomaki Kamabuko Fishcake 4 Slices
Roasted Nori 6 Sheets

Directions:

  1. Heat the sesame oil in a large pot over medium-low heat and add the onion, ginger, and garlic. Cook until just tender and fragrant, roughly 10 minutes. Add the ground pork, breaking the meat with a wooden spoon. Cook intil the meat is no longer pink, roughly 5 minutes, then stir in the miso paste until evenly distributed. Pour in the chicken stock, dashi stock, and mirin. Add the green onion and increase the heat to medium. Bring to a simmer, the reduce the heat back down to medium-low. Simmer for 10 minutes, skimming off foam as needed.
  2. Strain the soup through a fine mesh strainer into a large pitcher or pot, reserving the solids for rice or ramen toppings if desired. Season with salt and white pepper if desired.
  3. Bring a large pot of water to a boil and stir in the noodles. Cook according to the package directions, usually about 3 minutes, until the noodles are just tender and cooked all the way through. Drain but dont rinse and split into 2 serving bowls. Spoon broth over the noodles, then top each with 5 to 7 overlapping slices of chasu, menma, chopped green onion, 2 slices of narutomaki, 1 ajitsuke cut in half, and 3 sheets of nori.
