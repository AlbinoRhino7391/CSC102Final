Chasu Pork

Japanese Braised Pork

Needed to make Ichiraku Ramen

What you will need:

Ingredient Amount
Sake Rice Wine 1 Cup
Soy Sauce 1 Cup
Dark Brown Sugar 1/2 Cup
Leek 1 cut into 2 inch segments (Discard dark parts and roots)
Ginger 2 inch Piece unpeeled and thinly sliced
Skinless Pork Belly 1 Pound
Vegetable Oil 1 Tablespoon

Directions:

  1. In a large, deep pot over a high-heat combine 2 cups of water with the sake, soy sauce, sugar, leek, and ginger. Roll the pork belly tightly into a log and secure very snuggly with bakers twine in at least 5 equally spaced spots. Place a large skillet over high-heat and add the oil. Once the pan is very hot, sear the rolled meat well on all sides, 2-3 minutes per side. Transfer the meat to the deep pot and stir to coat with the marinade. Bring the marinade to a boil, skim off any foam, then reduce the heat to medium-low. Cover the pot and cook for 2 hours, rotating the meat every 20 minutes and skimming off the foam as needed.
  2. Remove the pot from the heat and let the meat cool in the marinade. Remove the meat from the pot and place in a sturdy zip-lock bag. Strain and reserve the marinating liquid, pouring enough into the bag to cover the meat. Refrigerate overnight. The leftover marinade can be used to make the menma and ajitsuke tamago ramen toppings which also marinade overnight.
  3. On the following day, preheat your oven to broil and line a baking sheet with aluminium foil. Remove the meat from the bag and discard any solidified fat bits. Cut the strings, and slice the meat into 1/4 inch thick slices. P;ace the slices on the baking sheet and broil for 2-3 minutes, just long enough to crisp the meat. Serve on top of ramen.
